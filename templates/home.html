What is a thought experiment?

A thought experiment is a hypothetical scenario created to explore a complex idea or question. These scenarios are designed to challenge our assumptions, reveal inconsistencies in our beliefs, and push us to think deeply about the world around us. It is similar to a scientific experiment but with things that cannot be or should not be (for ethical reasons) experimented on. Thought experiments allow the thinker to change variables, create a base case, and have unlimited participants.

What is philosophy?

Philosophy comes from the two Greek words philein and sophia, which means love of wisdom. Philosophy is a branch of study that seeks to understand fundamental questions about the nature of reality, existence, knowledge, values, ethics, and the human experience. It involves critical thinking, reasoning, and questioning in order to explore and understand concepts and ideas that shape our understanding of the world around us. Interestingly, most fields of study (math, physics, etc) started off as a part of philosophy. Today, there are four main branches: metaphysics (what is real), epistomology (how do we know what we know), ethics (what is right and wrong), and logic (how do we reason). Some people also include aesthetics, which is the discussion of beauty.
